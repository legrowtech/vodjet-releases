# VODJet – AI Twitch Clipper for Windows

VODJet is a Windows desktop app that helps gaming streamers turn long Twitch VODs into short-form clips for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts — all on your PC, without cloud uploads or monthly credits.

If you stream regularly and want to post clips faster, VODJet scans your recordings, finds the best moments, and exports ready‑to‑post vertical videos that you can upload directly to short‑form platforms.

---

## What VODJet does

- Turns long Twitch VODs into short clips for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts.  
- Auto‑detects highlight moments (kills, clutches, reactions, fails) in gaming streams.  
- Formats gameplay and facecam cleanly for 9:16 vertical video.  
- Adds burned‑in captions so clips work without sound.  
- Runs entirely on your Windows PC — no cloud uploads, no waiting, no credit system.  

VODJet is built for **solo streamers, mid‑tier creators, and anyone who wants to grow** by posting more often without spending hours editing.

---

## Who should use VODJet

- Twitch, Kick, or YouTube streamers who post to TikTok, Reels, or Shorts.  
- Competitive gamers (Valorant, Fortnite, League of Legends, Call of Duty, etc.) who want to highlight their best plays.  
- Anyone who records 4–8+ hour streams and wants a fast, repeatable way to clip highlights.

If you are tired of scrubbing through long VODs or waiting for cloud‑based clipping tools, VODJet is designed to speed up your workflow.

---

## How it works (simple version)

1. **Record** your stream as usual.  
2. **Open** VODJet on your Windows PC.  
3. **Drag** your VOD file into the app.  
4. **Draw** a box around your facecam and gameplay area.  
5. **Select** TikTok, Reels, or Shorts (all 9:16).  
6. **Generate** clips and let VODJet find the best moments.  
7. **Export** and upload the ready‑to‑post clips.

Most users get **5–10 clips from a 6–8 hour stream in under 20 minutes**.

- **Operating system:** Windows 10 or Windows 11.

- **Storage:** Needs enough space for your VODs and exported clips (large files are common for Twitch recordings).  
- **Other:** Runs locally — no web account required, just a Windows machine and your stream files.

3. Launch VODJet after install.  
4. Drag your Twitch VOD (`.mp4` or similar) into the app.
